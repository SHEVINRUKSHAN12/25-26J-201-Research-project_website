Year   Integrated Home Design and Planning Management System   
 
 
 CoEAI  - Centre of Excellence for AI  
Information Technology (IT)  
Creating a home is one of the most significant investments for individuals and families, 
particularly in developing countries such as Sri Lanka, where owning a house is often considered 
a lifelong achievement. However, the process of planning and building a house is highly complex 
and presents multiple challenges for prospective homeowners. The increasing demand for 
housing, especially in urban areas, has led to issues such as limited space, inefficient designs, and 
a lack of proper alignment with cultural practices and environmental needs. The core challenge 
lies in integrating modern design techniques with traditional cultural values while ensuring that 
the design is efficient, functional, and sustainable. This has been further exacerbated by the 
recent ec onomic crisis, which has led to a surge in construction costs and a decline in new 
projects [4].  
 
One of the primary issues homeowners face is interior space optimization. In many cases, 
traditional methods of space planning do not take full advantage of the available area, leading to 
wasted space and inefficient layouts. The current approach to home d esign is often fragmented, 
requiring homeowners to engage with multiple professionals —such as architects, engineers, and 
contractors —and to manually coordinate each stage of the project. This siloed process increases 
costs, causes delays, and significantly  raises the risk of miscommunication and errors [2]. 
Without a centralized, integrated platform, it is difficult for homeowners to visualize their project 
holistically, from initial concept to final construction plan, hindering their ability to make 
inform ed decisions.  
 
A second significant problem is the disconnection between modern design and traditional 
knowledge, specifically principles like Vastu Shastra. These principles, which are deeply rooted in  
Lankan culture, focus on creating Lankan system of architecture, emphasizes the importance of 
spatial alignment to promote harmony, energy flow, and well -being. Traditional designs often 
adhere strictly to Vastu principles, but modern homes frequently neglect these guidelines in 
favor of s tructural efficiency or aesthetic appeal.  This creates a disconnect between the 
functional and spiritual  aspects of the  25-26J-201

6. Brief description of the nature of the solution including a conceptual diagram (250 
words max)  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 The solution is an AI -powered platform designed to optimize residential home designs by 
improving both the functionality and efficiency of space while integrating Vastu Vidya principles 
for cultural alignment. The system begins by taking user inputs, inclu ding land dimensions and 
specific requirements like the number of rooms and outdoor space. Once this information is 
provided, the AI analyzes the layout and generates space optimization suggestions, ensuring 
efficient room usage and traffic flow.  
An additional feature allows users to align their design with Vastu Vidya, adjusting room 
orientations, furniture placements, and overall layout to promote spiritual harmony. For users 
working with smaller plots of land, the system offers recommendations f or land optimization, 
ensuring the most efficient use of the available space for both the house and garden.  
Furthermore, the system addresses exterior space optimization by maximizing the functionality 
of gardens, rooftops, and facades. Whether for relaxation, gardening, or energy -efficient 
designs, the system ensures these outdoor areas serve multiple purposes.  It also provides real -
time feedback and generates a compliance score, helping users see how well their designs 
adhere to both Vastu Vidya principles and space optimization goals. This comprehensive tool 
ensures that homes are not only efficient and functi onal but also culturally aligned and 
spiritually balanced.

4. Data Requirements: The system will need several types of data:  
• Floor plan data : Dimensions of rooms and furniture placements.  
• User preferences : Specific requirements for room functions, furniture types, 
and aesthetic choices.  
• Vastu Vidya guidelines : Cultural data on room placements and furniture 
orientations.  
• Land dimensions : Data on land plots for land optimization.  
 
 To successfully develop the AI -powered Integrated Home Design and Planning Management 
System, specialized knowledge and expertise are required in several areas, including artificial 
intelligence, architectural design, and cultural principles like Vastu Vid ya. 
1. Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning:  The system will rely heavily on AI and 
machine learning to analyze  floor plans, optimize space usage, and provide real -time 
feedback. Expertise in training AI models to detect inefficiencies in layout and suggest 
real-time improvements is essential. Additionally, AI will be used to align designs with 
Vastu Vidya principl es, requiring knowledge of rule -based AI to integrate cultural 
guidelines into the system.  
2. Architectural Design and Space Optimization:  Understanding space planning and 
ergonomic design principles is crucial. The AI system must analyze room dimensions 
and efficiently place furniture while considering factors like traffic flow, comfort, and 
space usage. Expertise in interior design and lan d optimization will help the system 
generate practical, efficient layouts, particularly for smaller plots.
