Project Scope

Project Background & Research Scope

Literature Survey

Creating a home is one of the most significant investments for individuals and families, particularly in developing countries such as Sri Lanka. However, the process is highly complex and presents multiple challenges. The increasing demand for housing in urban areas has led to limited space, inefficient designs, and a lack of proper alignment with cultural practices. The current approach to home design is often fragmented, requiring homeowners to manually coordinate with multiple professionals (architects, engineers, contractors). This siloed process increases costs, causes delays, and significantly raises the risk of errors.

Research Gap

🧩

Research Problem & Solution

⚠ Proposed Problem

How can we integrate modern design techniques with traditional cultural values while ensuring that residential design is efficient, functional, and sustainable, all without the high costs and disjointed workflows of the traditional architectural process?

✓ Proposed Solution

The solution is an AI-powered platform designed to optimize residential home designs. The system takes user inputs, analyzes the layout, and generates space optimization suggestions. It seamlessly aligns designs with Vastu Vidya (adjusting room orientation and furniture), optimizes land use for smaller plots, and maximizes the functionality of exterior spaces like gardens and rooftops. Real-time feedback and compliance scores help users make informed decisions.

Research Objectives

Methodology & Architecture

The system uses a modular yet integrated architecture combining land analysis, spatial optimization, cultural compliance, and visualization. It includes four modules: land utilization with 2D plan generation, AI-based Vastu detection, interior space optimization, and exterior space optimization. Modules communicate via centralized JSON data flows, sharing user inputs to ensure consistent planning.

YOLOv8 and CNN models are utilized for semantic segmentation and room detection, while Rule-based and Constraint-based methods optimize furniture placement and outdoor layout. A Genetic Algorithm (GA) evolves interior layouts by penalizing constraint violations and rewarding efficient space utilization.
