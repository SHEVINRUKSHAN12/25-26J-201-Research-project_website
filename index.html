Project Background & Research Scope

This section explains the problem context, research gap, proposed solution, methodology, and enabling technologies behind HomeScope.

01

Literature Survey

Creating a home is one of the most significant investments for individuals and families, particularly in developing countries such as Sri Lanka. However, the process is highly complex and presents multiple challenges. The increasing demand for housing in urban areas has led to limited space, inefficient designs, and a lack of proper alignment with cultural practices. The current approach to home design is often fragmented, requiring homeowners to manually coordinate with multiple professionals such as architects, engineers, and contractors. This siloed process increases costs, causes delays, and significantly raises the risk of errors.

02

Research Gap

Space

Inefficient Space Utilization

Traditional planning methods do not fully optimize available residential area, resulting in wasted layouts and underused spaces.

Culture

Cultural Disconnection

Modern generic design practices often overlook Vastu principles, weakening the relationship between functionality and cultural alignment.

Workflow

Fragmented Workflows

Without a unified platform, homeowners struggle to visualize and evaluate planning choices from concept through implementation.

03

Research Problem & Solution

Problem

How can home planning become efficient, explainable, and culturally aligned?

Current residential design workflows often remain expensive, disconnected, and difficult to optimize across land use, interior layout, exterior functionality, and cultural requirements.

Solution

HomeScope unifies planning intelligence inside a single AI-assisted workflow.

The platform analyzes user inputs, optimizes layouts, evaluates Vastu compliance, and improves both interior and exterior space utilization while helping users make better design decisions in real time.

05

Methodology & Architecture

The system uses a modular but integrated architecture combining land analysis, spatial optimization, cultural compliance, and visualization. It includes four modules: land utilization with 2D plan generation, AI-based Vastu detection, interior space optimization, and exterior space optimization.

Modules communicate through centralized JSON data flows to maintain planning consistency. YOLOv8 and CNN models are used for semantic segmentation and room detection, while rule-based, constraint-based, and genetic algorithm approaches improve space usage, furniture placement, and outdoor layout quality.
