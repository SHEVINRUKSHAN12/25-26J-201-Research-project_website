Abstract—Modern house planning is complex due to
limited land, space constraints, and cultural requirements,
often requiring costly expert input. This research proposes
an AI-powered home planning system integrating land
utilization, automated 2D plan generation, interior and
exterior optimization, and Vastu compliance evaluation. It
uses YOLOv8 and CNN models for room detection, along-
side rule- and constraint-based optimization for layouts
and furniture placement. Operating on structured JSON
data, the system ensures modular integration and efficient
processing. Experiments show it provides a cost- and
timeefficient solution, offering intelligent decision support
without expert intervention.
Keywords—Artificial Intelligence, Smart Home Design,
Floor Plan Generation, Space Optimization, Vastu Com-
pliance.
I. INTRODUCTION
Residential planning faces challenges from urbaniza-
tion, irregular land, and regulations, especially in de-
veloping countries, increasing costs and planning time.
Traditional workflows, including land analysis, floor plan
design, interior/exterior layouts, and cultural compli-
ance, are fragmented, causing poor space utilization and
repeated revisions. While AI (YOLO, CNN) enables
room detection [3], [4] and rule-/constraint-based meth-
ods optimize furniture [5], [6], exterior planning andVastu compliance remain manual [7]. We propose a
unified system with four modules (land utilization and
2D plan generation, AI-based Vastu inspection, interior
space optimization, and exterior planning) combining
deep learning, rule-based, and constraint-based methods
on structured data for efficient, cost-effective planning.
II. RELATED WORK
A. Automated Land Planning and Floor Plan Generation
Early rule-based approaches [8], [9] lack flexibility
for complex land, while GAN methods [10] ignore land

frameworks.
C. Interior Space Optimization and Furniture Arrange-

Rule- and constraint-based methods optimize circula-

treats interiors in isolation.D. Exterior Space Planning and Cultural Compliance
Outdoor planning research focuses on zoning and

E. Summary of Research Gaps
No fully integrated system combines land-use op-
timization, floor plan generation, interior/exterior opti-
mization, and cultural compliance, highlighting the need
for a unified AI-based residential planning framework.
III. METHODOLOGY
A. Overall System Architecture
The system uses a modular yet integrated architecture
combining land analysis, spatial optimization, cultural
compliance, and visualization. It includes four mod-
ules: land utilization with 2D plan generation, AI-based
Vastu detection, interior space optimization, and exterior
space optimization. Modules communicate via central-
ized JSON data flows, sharing user inputs to ensure
consistent planning. Image inputs are used for visual
analysis (floor plans, room detection), processed with
metadata. The workflow generates optimized land use,
2D floor plans, interior and exterior layouts, and a Vastu
compliance report.

D. Interior Space Optimization Module
The interior space optimization module generates effi-
cient furniture layouts for rectangular and square rooms
using a genetic algorithm (GA) that encodes furniture
positions, orientations, and spacing as chromosomes.
The GA evolves layouts through selection, crossover,
and mutation, penalizing constraint violations, while a
lightweight ML model provides heuristic guidance based
on learned layout patterns.Fig. 3. Processing pipeline of the AI-based Vastu detection and
compliance evaluation module
1) Fitness Function Definition:The fitness function
enforces hard constraints and rewards desirable spatial
arrangements. Constraint violations (furniture outside
boundaries or overlapping) return negative penalties:
1000 for boundary violations and 500 times the overlap-
ping area for collisions. Valid layouts receive 10 points
per furniture item positioned within 0.1 meters of the
nearest wall, encouraging efficient space utilization.
To incorporate learned aesthetic patterns, geometric
fitness is combined with an ML-based heuristic score:
Ftotal(L) = 0.7×F geo(L) + 0.3×S ML(L)(1)
whereF geo(L)is the geometric fitness score,S ML(L)
is a neural network output predicting layout quality from
spatial features (room area, aspect ratio, furniture count,
centroid distribution), and the weighting factors (0.7,
0.3) prioritize constraint satisfaction while allowing ML-
guided aesthetic refinement.

E. Exterior Space Optimization Module
Functional outdoor areas (parking, pathways, land-
scaping, open spaces) are allocated via rule-based spatial

and efficient land use.

spaces, and assess Vastu compliance, focusing on func-

usability rather than isolated accuracy metrics.

space-constrained parcels, and varied user requirements

plans for all tested land configurations, dynamically
adjusting room allocations when requirements exceeded
land limits, preserving functional integrity.
Layouts maintained logical room arrangements, cir-
culation, and boundary compliance. JSON-based data
exchange ensured seamless integration with subsequent
modules, demonstrating efficient modular interoperabil-
ity.

V. DISCUSSION
The framework supports data-driven decision-making
through integrated land utilization, floor plan generation,
interior and exterior optimization, and Vastu compli-
ance evaluation. The modular design enables coordinated
planning and reduces fragmented manual workflows.
Current limitations include reliance on 2D representa-
tions and rule-based cultural evaluation. Future work will
focus on 3D visualization, adaptive learning-based op-
timization, and validation using real-world architectural
data.
VI. CONCLUSION
This study presents a modular AI-based smart home
design system combining land utilization, automated 2D
floor plan generation, interior and exterior optimization,
and Vastu compliance. Structured JSON data flows en-
able efficient module interaction and scalability. Experi-
mental results show the system produces feasible, opti-
mized residential layouts under varying land constraints,
reducing time and expert reliance, offering a practical
and cost-effective solution for space- or culture-sensitive
planning.
